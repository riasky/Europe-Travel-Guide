Western Europe

In Western Europe, several countries attract a significant number of tourists due to their cultural heritage, landmarks, and natural beauty. Some of the most touristic countries in Western Europe include:

France: Known for the iconic Eiffel Tower in Paris, the French Riviera, historic landmarks like the Louvre Museum, and picturesque regions like Provence and Normandy.

United Kingdom: Home to London's famous landmarks such as Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, historical sites like Stonehenge, scenic landscapes in Scotland, and cultural attractions in cities like Edinburgh and Bath.

Spain: Renowned for Barcelona's architecture, Madrid's cultural offerings, the vibrant culture of Andalusia, stunning beaches in Costa del Sol, and historical sites like Alhambra.

Italy: Famous for Rome's ancient history and landmarks like the Colosseum, Venice's canals, Florence's art and architecture, the Amalfi Coast's scenic beauty, and historical sites such as Pompeii.

Netherlands: Known for Amsterdam's canals, historic sites like Anne Frank House, colorful tulip fields, windmills, and the vibrant culture and art scene.

Belgium: Famous for Brussels' Grand Place, Bruges' medieval architecture, delicious chocolates, waffles, and the historic city of Ghent.

Ireland: Renowned for Dublin's lively atmosphere, the scenic landscapes of the Cliffs of Moher, historic castles like Blarney Castle, and the cultural richness of cities like Galway.
